# Axioms

The floor of CC-PRO. Every composition must pass all five.

## I. Type is the hero.

Typography — not photography, not color, not illustration — drives hierarchy. Imagery is auxiliary. Color is a sharpener, not a decorator.

If removing the imagery breaks the page, you're doing it wrong.

## II. Tension is beauty.

Near-symmetry, not perfect symmetry. Deliberate imbalance. The eye finds perfection flat and imperfection alive.

Offset your grid by ~1.15×. Break it at one load-bearing point (see Weingart persona).

## III. Whitespace is pressurized.

Negative space pushes elements apart with intent. It is not what's left over — it is what you PUT there.

Pressure creates meaning. A single word floating in a sea of cream is a different message than that word crammed next to twelve others.

## IV. Motion has meaning.

Every animation answers: *what problem does this solve for the user?* If the answer is "because it looks cool," cut it.

Motion signals interactivity, establishes hierarchy, provides feedback, manages attention. See `principles/motion.md` for the four categories.

## V. Derive, don't decorate.

Aesthetic DNA — palette, typographic voice, motion character — is **derived** per-run from the subject matter. Not picked from a menu.

The subject already contains its own design system. Your job is to listen for it.

---

## Corollary — The Violation

Every composition contains exactly one deliberate violation of its own chosen mode. The violation is **load-bearing** — if you removed it, the composition would be poorer.

This is not decoration. This is not "the discord color." This is a structural move that demonstrates you know the rules deeply enough to break them.

(See: Wolfgang Weingart. "You can only break the rules if you understand them first.")

## Corollary — The Signature

Every output has exactly one signature microfeature (see `principles/microfeatures.md`). Not more.

The craft-read comes from restraint. Three microfeatures is a template; one is a decision.

## Corollary — The Discipline

The axioms are the floor. The personas are the ceiling. The floor is universal; the ceiling changes per run.

A Saville-mode composition and a Weingart-mode composition look nothing alike — but both pass all five axioms, or neither ships.
