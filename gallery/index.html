Four pieces of work. Four annotations. Four stances on what "award-level" means in 2026. The gallery exists to be studied, not copied — its purpose is to raise the floor of every future output that reads it, not to be matched.

The rule

Every exemplar is analyzed along four axes — palette, layout, motion, type — with explicit annotation of what to carry forward and what NOT to carry forward. The skill's quality gate compares any generated output against this archive and rejects when similarity exceeds 0.7 on any axis.

In practice: if your output looks like any of the work below, regenerate.

No. 01 Presentation · Live deck

Vibe Coding Nights · ClawCamp Rayyan Zahid & Michalis Vasileiadis
San Francisco, April 2026

Weingart-adjacent hybrid

A 48-slide deck that choreographs ember particles across a room.

The strongest original move is the two-phase motion architecture — embers drift, converge to focal targets (~1900ms), dwell, then optionally fracture outward. Wave/cohesion timing: each particle's delay is index × 18ms, creating visual rhythm per-particle rather than group-simultaneous. Data-attribute-driven motion config means no JS tuning required.

The palette ( blaze amber on charcoal ) was right for this subject — fire, claws, heat — but is not portable. PRO must derive per-run, not pick from v1's named moods.

No. 02 Therapist site · Cinematic scroll

Maxima Therapy Louis Paquet
React + GSAP + Matter.js
2026

Saville-adjacent, punctuating

A 50-point physics rope as the page's load-bearing signature.

The one-signature-motion-move rule in practice. Matter.js-driven rope, draggable, ripple propagates along the line — and nothing else on the page competes. Every other motion beat is scroll-scrubbed restraint. This is what "ONE signature, everything else utility" looks like when a studio commits to it.

PRO carries forward the rule, not the rope. Every subject needs its own load-bearing physics moment — or none at all.

No. 03 Driver portfolio · Awwwards SOTY 2025

Lando Norris OFF+BRAND (studio)
2025

Scher-leading, Bierut framing

A two-tone discipline with one loud accent reserved like ammunition.

Off-white, charcoal, and a single lime green that appears only on interactive moments and one or two brand-payoff beats. Every other surface is quiet. The single loud note becomes an event — which is the entire argument against Tailwind-default accent peppering.

Each major section shifts the palette (race → lime, portrait → black, career → silver-on-navy), matching the chaptered nature of an F1 career. The scroll feels like turning magazine pages. Chromatic section shifts, done right.

No. 04 Rebrand · 2007 · Pentagram

Saks Fifth Avenue Michael Bierut, Pentagram
Based on Massimo Vignelli's
1973 script

The Bierut canon

One calligraphic script, split into 64 squares, used as a generative identity.

Bierut took Vignelli's 1973 Saks wordmark and decomposed it into a 64-square grid. Every shopping bag, sign, label, and ad was composed from a different fragment combination — sometimes rotated, sometimes scaled. One source, 64 pieces, effectively infinite identity. The constraint produced coherence; the decomposition produced variety.

This is the canonical example of adaptive reuse. The Bierut persona in CC-PRO derives its entire method from this work: audit heritage, find the fragment with good bones, deconstruct, recombine, assert the present.
