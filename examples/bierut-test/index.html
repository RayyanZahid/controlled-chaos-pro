v1→PRO

An adaptive reuse · Bierut-mode · 2026

01
Audit

What v1 already owned.

Controlled Chaos v1 shipped with five aesthetic modes, eight mood palettes, five typographic voices, a continuous forces framework, and an axiom set. Before PRO, we audited every asset for structural integrity.

  • 200 combinations→5 authorial methods
  • lookup-by-menu→derivation-by-subject
  • 8 named moods→palette machinery
  • 5 voice presets→composition primitives
02
Kept

The five continuous forces.

The forces abstraction — five 0-to-1 spectrums (structure, density, warmth, stillness, volume) that derive every downstream CSS value — was v1's strongest original contribution. PRO preserves it entirely. The spectrums survived; the named preset list that picked five positions on them did not.

The axioms also carry forward, with one addition. Axiom V — derive, don't decorate — is new to PRO and governs everything above it.

03
Transformed

From menu to method.

The deepest transformation: CC v1 asked designers to pick a mode. PRO asks them to pick a persona — Saville, Scher, Rams, Weingart, Bierut. A mode is an aesthetic. A persona is a working process. One is a destination, the other is a map.

The ambition isn't aesthetic variety (you can get that from any generator). The ambition is methodological variety — outputs that differ because they were produced by different decisions, not different lookups.

04
Removed

The menu.

200 preset combinations across modes, moods, and voices — removed in full. The gallery remains, but as inspiration only; the similarity-reject quality gate stops new outputs from matching it. The skill no longer answers the question "which combination?" — it answers "derived from what, authored how?"

The transformation is the method, and the method is the identity.
