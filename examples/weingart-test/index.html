01 · Essay

Weingart at forty: what Basel taught the browser

Forty years after Weingart layered photographs through typographic grids at Basel, the browser has caught up. CSS clip-path, mix-blend-mode, and SVG filters now do what halftone plates and offset registration once did — badly.

02 · Case study

Greiman's “Snow White and the Seven Pixels” revisited

April Greiman's 1986 3M poster predicted the variable font. The principle was the same: rules layered across rules until pattern emerged. The web has had variable fonts since 2017 and still treats them as a gimmick.

03 · Technique

Chromatic aberration as meaning, not decoration

A 2px RGB channel split is a craft-read. A 12px split is broken. The principle: the violation must be legible as intentional. Push beyond that and it reads as a render bug.
