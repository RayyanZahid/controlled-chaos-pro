# Register

> *"The painting is Mary Oliver. The pitch deck is the electrician."*

Persona is the designer's method. Register is the writing voice. They are orthogonal — a Saville composition can be written in editorial register or commercial register, and both are legitimate.

Most CC-PRO output is **editorial** by default. That's correct for subjects whose job is to move a reader — manifestos, tributes, exhibits, brand pages, atmospheric landing surfaces.

**Commercial register** exists for subjects whose job is to close a transaction — pitch decks, quotes, sales pages, investor one-pagers, event proposals. These surfaces are read by someone who has ten minutes, a budget, and a decision to make. Literary voice, in that seat, reads as either evasive or amateur.

## When to switch to commercial

Set `register: commercial` when ANY of:

- The subject is a **pitch** — for a round, a deal, an event, a partnership
- The reader is **deciding**, not feeling — they have a checkbook or a calendar
- The output includes **pricing, dates, capacity, or SLAs** — facts with dollar values attached
- The brief names **"client-facing," "B2B," "quote," "proposal," "pitch deck"**

Stay with editorial register (the default, no declaration needed) when:

- The subject's job is to move the reader emotionally
- The output is a manifesto, tribute, exhibit, or brand page
- The reader came to feel something, not decide something

The test: *could this output be mistaken for an essay?* If yes, editorial is right. If the output needs to be mistaken for a quote or a spec, commercial is right.

## What commercial register enforces

Commercial register is defined by six prohibitions and six requirements. They fix the failure modes that literary voice produces when it's pointed at a transactional surface.

### Prohibited moves

**1. Meta-commentary.** Sentences that narrate the deck's structure or the reader's journey. *"Ordered hero-first."* *"An option, not a pitch."* *"We don't need a big process."* Meta-commentary signals that the author is thinking about the document instead of thinking about the reader's problem.

**2. Narrative framing of the reader's experience.** *"You chose X."* *"The staircase carries you down."* *"Descent into deeper rooms."* The reader hasn't chosen anything yet — they're reading a PDF. Writing as if the experience has already begun is a literary move that reads, on a pitch surface, as presumptuous.

**3. Romantic or poetic phrasing.** *"One event is a transaction; four is a relationship."* *"The grammar your product runs on."* These are essay sentences. An essay earns them by having a reader who opted in to 2,000 words. A pitch does not.

**4. Self-undercutting language on tiers.** *"Test-drive."* *"Start here before anything bigger."* *"Low-friction."* Each tier is a real offering with real value. Describing the entry tier as a trial run for the expensive tier is upsell language, and it signals the author doesn't believe in the entry tier's value on its own.

**5. Artificial curation.** *"A carefully chosen pair of X and Y"* when all options are actually available. *"Our curated lineup"* when the real answer is "everything we make." Cap-setting is a literary narrowing move; on a pitch surface it reads as hiding the menu.

**6. Framing-as-claim.** Saying something IS true because the author has asserted it — *"the descent narrative your product runs on,"* *"the relationship tier."* Contrast with stating what IS because it IS: *"16,000 sqft, 2 levels, 14 rooms."* Facts don't need rhetorical scaffolding. If a claim needs framing to survive, it isn't load-bearing.

### Required moves

**1. Fact-first sentences.** Lead with what IS before what it MEANS. *"$18,000, four rooms, three hours."* before *"A full buyout of the venue."* The reader's attention budget is spent on the first clause; put the load-bearing number there.

**2. Direct asks.** *"Pick a date."* *"Lock in four events."* *"Sign the MSA and we start next week."* Never *"Four events across a year is a relationship"* — that's a hint dressed as a metaphor. The ask is a verb in the imperative, not an implication.

**3. Preserve tier value.** Each offering stands on its own. The $500/person tier is not a "test drive" for the $50,000 buyout; it's a $500/person tier. If you can't describe it without comparing it unfavorably to something more expensive, the tier itself is underspecced.

**4. Uncapped optionality.** *"All our experiences are available."* *"Mix and match across the catalog."* Not *"a curated pair of X and Y."* The reader chooses the cap; the author's job is to show the full menu.

**5. Plural customer claims.** *"Tech's favorite private venue in SF."* Not *"23 separate Googlers have booked events with us."* Plural-noun framing is stronger than counted-individual framing; the exception is a specific logo wall or named case study, which IS strong.

**6. Pricing without apology.** State the number. Move on. No *"starting at,"* no *"as low as,"* no *"depending on scope."* If the number needs a paragraph of softening, the number is wrong; fix the number, don't pad the sentence.

## Design-level implications

Register is primarily a copy discipline, but it has typographic consequences. Editorial register and commercial register want different type furniture:

| Element | Editorial | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| Section eyebrows | Small-caps serif or grotesque, wide-tracked | Plain numbered headers (01, 02, 03) or sans-serif all-caps at body size |
| Captions | Italic serif, hanging-indent | Mono sans or plain sans at caption size |
| Pull-quotes | Large italic serif, pulled into margin | Callout boxes with a stat + one-line claim |
| Photo captions | Italic serif, pulled-in | Mono or sans, or omitted entirely |
| Running heads | Small-caps editorial | Document metadata (page N of M, client name, date) |
| Aside / footnote rules | Hairline ornamental | Solid rule or box |

The commercial palette of type furniture is the one pitch decks, legal docs, product specs, and financial reports all converge toward. It's not uglier than editorial furniture — it signals *I respect your ten minutes* instead of *I am asking for your Saturday afternoon.*

Forces tend to shift with register too. Commercial compositions typically sit at higher **structure** (0.85–1.0), lower **warmth** (0.1–0.4), higher **stillness** (0.8–1.0), and moderate **volume** (0.4–0.7). They're closer to Rams-territory on the forces map regardless of which persona was selected — because the reader came to decide, not to feel.

## Register across personas

Register modifies output; it does not change persona. Every persona can operate in either register.

| Persona | Editorial default | Commercial variant |
|---|---|---|
| **Saville** | Editorial is the native register. Anchor-driven, considered, essay-adjacent. | Anchor still drives palette and type, but copy is fact-first. The anchor is framed, not narrated. A pitch deck for a heritage brand uses Saville + commercial when the goal is the contract, not the tribute. |
| **Scher** | Civic-scale proposition as thesis. *"EVERY PAGE IS ONE OF ONE."* | Civic-scale proposition as offer. *"FOUR EVENTS. ONE PRICE. PICK DATES."* Scale stays; romantic phrasing doesn't. |
| **Rams** | Rams is already fact-first; editorial Rams is functional product pages. | Commercial Rams is the dominant choice for pitch decks. Rams's method IS commercial register, rendered visually. This is why Rams + commercial is the default recommendation for any brief where the reader is deciding. |
| **Weingart** | Disciplined Swiss + one expressive violation, for design-literate audiences. | The violation must carry commercial weight — a pricing block rotated, a stat scaled 3× — not a romantic flourish. |
| **Bierut** | Adaptive reuse of heritage, narrative sequence. | Heritage is used as credibility (logo lineage, numbered identity marks), not as a tribute. The transformation becomes a proof-point. |

Rams is the closest persona to commercial register by default. If the brief is transactional and no specific heritage, anchor, or civic-scale phrase presents itself, **Rams + commercial** is the right first choice.

## Quality gate for commercial register

Before shipping a commercial-register composition, scan for failure signals:

- [ ] No phrase like *"a transaction,"* *"a relationship,"* *"not a pitch,"* *"test-drive,"* *"low-friction,"* *"starting at,"* *"carefully chosen"*
- [ ] Every tier description could stand alone if the other tiers were deleted
- [ ] Pricing appears as a number, not as a softened phrase
- [ ] Dates, capacities, and quantities are in digits — not spelled out
- [ ] No sentence narrates what the deck is doing or what the reader is experiencing
- [ ] Asks are verbs in the imperative, not implications
- [ ] Claims about the customer base are plural-noun, not counted-individual (unless a named case study)

If any check fails, the copy has drifted back toward editorial. Rewrite the offending sentence to lead with its fact.

**On availability**

> *After:* All 14 experiences available. Mix across rooms. Swap between sessions.

**On the deck's own structure**
> *Before:* Ordered hero-first, with optional tiers below. Not a pitch — an option.
> *After:* Four tiers. Pricing below. Pick one.

**On venue description**
> *Before:* The staircase carries you down into deeper rooms, the descent narrative your product runs on.
> *After:* 16,000 sqft. Two levels. 14 rooms.

**On customer proof**
> *Before:* 23 separate Googlers have booked events with us over the last 18 months.
> *After:* Tech's favorite private venue in SF. Google, Stripe, Anthropic on the list.

**On the ask**
> *Before:* Low-friction entry point — test-drive the format before anything bigger.
> *After:* Start with one event. Book at [url].

Five sentences × two registers. Each commercial version loses nothing the reader needs; each editorial version adds something the reader doesn't want on a pitch surface.

## Register is orthogonal

A composition declares persona AND register. They combine freely:

- *Saville + editorial* — the default for heritage tributes, cultural pages (Factory Records example)
- *Saville + commercial* — a heritage brand's pitch deck (keep the anchor, strip the narration)
- *Rams + editorial* — rare; functional product page with one italic editorial sentence as the discord
- *Rams + commercial* — the default for pitch decks, quotes, spec sheets
- *Scher + editorial* — a manifesto page, civic-scale thesis
- *Scher + commercial* — an offer page, civic-scale number (*"$18,000. FOUR ROOMS. THREE HOURS."*)

Register is declared in the brief OR inferred from Phase 1b signals (see `composition-engine.md` Phase 1b for the inference rule). When in doubt, ask one question.
